Creighton University Summer Research Institute

This past summer, I had the privilege of working with the department of Chemistry and Biochemistry laboratory through Creighton University’s Summer Research Institute. It has been an honor to collaborate with esteemed colleagues to shine a light on critical insights into a disease called Carnitine palmitoyltransferase deficiency II, an area that lacked sufficient research. I extend my deepest gratitude to my mentor, Dr Eric J Haas for introducing me to this issue and for being such an amazing individual to collaborate under. His pivotal role in fostering personal and team development throughout this project has been invaluable. On Friday, July 26th, I had the opportunity to present our team's research findings regarding " Altered Lipid Metabolism in the Fall Armyworm, Spodoptera frugiperda" at Creighton's Summer Research Institute Colloquium and was awarded Honorable Mention. This event would not have been possible without the dedication and time commitment that Dr. Sade Kosoko-Lasaki and Razia Aziz-Seible, PhD MBA, invested into this origination. Their efforts have provided young, talented students invaluable opportunities to give back to the community that has supported them. I am extremely blessed to have had the chance to work with many amazing students and mentors on discovering a cure to this the mysterious disease and spreading an awareness on such an underlying issue.
